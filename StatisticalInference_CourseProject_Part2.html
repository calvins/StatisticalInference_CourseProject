Synopsis

In this report, we’ll analyze a data set describing the length of teeth (measured in microns) in 60 guinea pigs who received Vitamin C using two different methods (orange juice or ascorbic acid) and three different dosage levels (0.5, 1, and 2 mg). We’ll form some hypothesis tests and compute confidence intervals to compare the effects of Vitamin C on tooth length. Specifically, we’ll compare the supplement method VC vs OJ and the different dosage comparisons for each supplement: 0.5 vs 1.0, 0.5 vs 2.0, and 1.0 vs 2.0.

Some questions we may want to ask are: Does OJ supplements increase teeth length more than VC supplements? Does 1.0 doses of Vitamin C increase teeth length more than 0.5 doses? Does 2.0 doses of Vitamin C increase teeth length more than 0.5 doses? Does 2.0 doses of Vitamin C increase teeth length more than 1.0 doses?

Confidence Intervals and tests

using the sample mean of VC and OJ, we’ll calculate the confidence interval. 68% 1 se 95% of values within 2 standard errors of the sample mean 99.7 3 se

conditions for the confidence interval -sampled observations must be independent -sample size n >= 30 or larger if population distribution is skewed -show normal or assume normal When the sample size is small and it’s difficult to confirm normality of the sample distribution, we use the t distribution. It’s always centered at 0 and has the single parameter degrees of freedom. As degrees of freedom increases, the t distribution starts to resemble a normal distribution. For inference on a mean, use t statistic when sample size n < 30 and the standard deviation is unknown.

unknown population mean can be estimated with the sample mean, t distribution, t statistic, and sample standard deviation which estimates the standard error.

Results

Conclusions

There is enough statistical evidence to conclude that guinea pig teeth length increases more when using supplement type OJ.

Additionally, increasing the dosages increases the teeth length, but the as the dosages get larger, the effect on teeth length is not as great as lower dosages.
